# DuPont Decomposition — Interpretation Templates For Six Patterns

Each pattern: identifying signal → interpretation → real example.

## Pattern 1: High ROE, good quality (peak)

Three components:
- Net margin: **high** (> 15%)
- Asset turnover: **moderate** (0.7-1.0x)
- Leverage: **low-to-moderate** (1.3-1.7x)

Interpretation: Strong competitive advantage (high margin) + efficient asset use
+ safe leverage. This is the **highest-quality ROE** pattern.

Example HPG 2021: margin 22.9% × turnover 0.91 × leverage 1.60 = ROE 33.5%. A
steel-cycle peak.

## Pattern 2: Low ROE, falling net margin (cyclical trough)

Three components:
- Net margin: **low** (< 8%)
- Asset turnover: stable
- Leverage: stable

Interpretation: The ROE decline comes **entirely from net margin** (selling
prices down or input costs up). Turnover and leverage stable → operating quality
is fine; this is a **sector-cycle issue**, not a structural one.

Example HPG 2023: margin 5.7% × turnover 0.67 × leverage 1.63 = ROE 6.3%. Global
HRC prices fell sharply.

Valuation insight: For cyclical stocks this can be a **buy point** — ROE recovers
when sector prices rebound.

## Pattern 3: ROE rising via leverage (warning)

Three components:
- Net margin: stable or slightly down
- Asset turnover: stable
- Leverage: **rising fast** (> 2.5x)

Interpretation: ROE up but **quality down** — the company borrows more to offset
falling margins. Financial risk rises. **Warning**; check:
- Net debt / EBITDA > 4x?
- Interest coverage (EBIT / interest) < 3x?
- Persistently negative investing cash flow?

Example (hypothetical): A real-estate firm borrowing to expand projects — ROE 18%
but leverage 3.5x. Low quality.

## Pattern 4: Stable ROE, stable components (defensive)

Three components:
- All three barely move over five years

Interpretation: A defensive stock with stable operations. Suitable for 5-year
average PE/PB valuation (stable multiples).

Example Vinamilk (VNM): ROE stable 20-25%, net margin 15-18%, leverage 1.2-1.4x.

## Pattern 5: Asset turnover falling sharply (capitalizing)

Three components:
- Asset turnover: **falling** from 1.0x to 0.5-0.7x
- Net margin and leverage stable

Interpretation: The company is **capitalizing heavily** — large fixed-asset
investment (furnaces, plants, real-estate projects). Total assets grow faster
than revenue. ROE falls temporarily.

Example HPG 2024-2025: turnover falls from 0.91 (2021) → 0.66 (2025) as Dung Quat
2 moves work-in-progress into fixed assets.

Insight: Recovers when the project ramps up and generates revenue.

## Pattern 6: ROE rising via margin recovery (high quality)

Three components:
- Net margin: **rising** (off a cyclical trough)
- Turnover and leverage: stable

Interpretation: The **best recovery pattern** — ROE rises from the most
sustainable source (profit per revenue). The stock may be at an accumulation
point.

Example HPG 2024-2025: net margin 5.7% (2023) → 8.6% (2024) → 9.8% (2025). ROE
recovers 6.3% → 10.7%. With P/B 1.25x (below 5y median 1.27x) → **buy point**.

## Decision-Tree Summary

```
ROE falling?
├── Net margin down, other two stable → SECTOR CYCLE → buy point for cyclical
├── Leverage up → QUALITY WARNING → check debt/EBITDA
└── Turnover down → CAPITALIZING → recovers when project completes

ROE high (> 20%)?
├── Net margin high (> 15%) → GOOD QUALITY → 5y median PE/PB valuation
├── Leverage high (> 2.5x) → ROE ILLUSION → discount valuation
└── Turnover high (> 1.5x) → ASSET-LIGHT (retail, services)
```
